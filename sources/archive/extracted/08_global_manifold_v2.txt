                                                  Abstract
          We present ”Global Manifold V2,” a risk management framework that evolves from independent
      instrument analysis to a systemic ”Zero Sum” approach. By aggregating ”Hazard”—an information-
      theoretic measure of structural unpredictability—across the G7 currency universe, we identify global
      regimes of ”Risk On/Off.” Empirical audits of the V1 strategy (2024–2025) revealed a statistically
      fragile local edge (Z-Score +1.23). However, a Global Correlation Study confirmed strong systemic
      linkage (Avg Corr 0.515). The V2 strategy, employing a Global Hazard Gate, improves average trade
      expectancy by filtering out high-entropy regimes where local signals are unreliable.


1     Introduction
The ”Zero Sum” hypothesis posits that in a closed system like the G7 FX market, structural energy
(predictability) is conserved. When one pair enters a chaotic (”High Hazard”) state, it often signals a
systemic rupture rather than an idiosyncratic event. This paper details the transition from V1 (Inde-
pendent Bundles) to V2 (Global Manifold).

3.2     Regime Taxonomy
Based on the historical distribution of ΛG , we define three V2 regimes:
    1. GREEN (Safe): ΛG ≤ 0.48. Structure is stable; local signals are trusted.

    3. RED (Chaotic): ΛG > 0.52. Systemic rupture; all signals are blocked (Capital Preservation).

5.1     Analysis
The V2 Global Manifold strategy succeeded in demonstrating structural selectivity. By filtering for
Global Safety (ΛG ≤ 0.48) and Quality in Transition (0.48 < ΛG ≤ 0.52, Coherence > 0.6), the system
improved the average trade quality by +35.66 basis points.
   However, the rigorous ”Red Regime” quarantine (active 15.8% of the time) and the ”Yellow Regime”
coherence filter (active on the majority of data) resulted in a significant reduction in trade volume (-
75.5%). While the Total PnL decreased, the Risk-Adjusted Return (Expectancy) increased, validating
the core hypothesis: Global Entropic State is a superior predictor of trade outcome than local
signals alone.

The V2 ”Global Manifold” strategy successfully mitigates the fragility of the V1 approach. By respecting
